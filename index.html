As a passionate researcher, I have engaged in various projects that bridge the fields of psychology, AI, and human-computer interaction. Below are some highlights of my research experience:

INFINITUS AI

Capstone Project Intern
Currently Working On

As part of my ongoing graduate capstone project, I have been working with INFINITUS AI, a company that specializes in developing advanced conversational AI solutions to health insurance follow up calls. In my role, I conduct experiments with diverse participant groups to evaluate the effectiveness of different AI agent versions in real-world applications. These experiments focused on collecting both quantitative and qualitative feedback on AI agent usability and likability.

I also analyzed user interactions and feedback to refine the conversational AI features, adjusting the tone, responsiveness, and overall engagement of the AI agents. These improvements lead to better user satisfaction metrics and a more seamless user experience, highlighting my ability to integrate human-centered design principles with AI technologies.

UNIVERSITY OF CALIFORNIA, SANTA CRUZ

Research Assistant
August 2022 - June 2023
Clinical Psychology Lab, Professor Hannah Raila

At UC Santa Cruz, I collaborated with Professor Hannah Raila in the clinical psychology lab, where we conducted research on the intersection of emotion, cognition, and psychopathology. A key aspect of my work was designing and programming a novel study that utilized the Continuous Flash Suppression (CFS) methodology. This study was focused on assessing visual awareness in individuals with contamination Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD).

My responsibilities included conducting extensive literature reviews, programming experiments using Matlab & Python, and creating surveys in Qualtrics to collect feedback on visual stimuli. This research helped improve our understanding of how individuals with OCD process and react to visual stimuli under suppressed awareness conditions.
